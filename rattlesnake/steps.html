Three wires are used in this step: The longest wire, the shortest wire, and a medium-length wire.

Connect the longest wire from the ultrasonic sensor in the head of the snake to port 1 on the NXT, routing it carefully through the neck of the snake as shown.

Connect the shortest wire from the neck motor to port B on the NXT.

Connect the medium-length wire from the second (tail) motor to port C on the NXT.

Design the coiled body of the snake any way you like (this is just for decoration).  Here is one example.

You can attach your snake body starting at the tail rattle and then wrapping loosely around the NXT brick.  You can peg the coils of your snake body to the four angled platform beams for stability.

Rattlesnake Programming

Use the program Rattlesnake for the Rattlesnake.  This is a fairly simple program that shows how the NXT-G programming system's "Sequence Beam" can be used to make two parts of a program do two different things at the same time.

 

Challenges

  • Because of the way the ultrasonic sensor is angled when the snake is coiled up, it is looking a little too far straight up and does not always see you if you just walk by in front of it.  Can you modify the design of the head to make the "eyes" work better?

  • Try modifying the Rattlesnake program to add sound or something else interesting you might think of.
